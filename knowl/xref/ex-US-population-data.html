Each row (corresponding to the population in a given year) represents a distinct state of the system. The state variables for our population include \(Y\text{,}\) the year of the observation, \(P\text{,}\) the population at the beginning of the indicated year, \(B\text{,}\) the total number of births recorded during that year, and \(D\text{,}\) the total number of deaths recorded during the year. We will also introduce a calculated variable \(t\) with \(t=Y-1990\text{,}\) so that \(t=1\) corresponds to the year \(Y=1991\text{.}\) This is done for convenience so that the numbers used when thinking about time are smaller in magnitude.
